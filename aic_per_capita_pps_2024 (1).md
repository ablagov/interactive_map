# Actual Individual Consumption per Capita in € PPS (2024)

**Source:** Eurostat [prc_ppp_ind] | **Data extracted:** January 2025

AIC (Actual Individual Consumption) measures material welfare — all goods and services households actually consume, regardless of who pays (households, government, or nonprofits).

## Key Insights

1. **Bulgaria's real welfare is 74% of EU average** — not the 45% suggested by nominal GDP comparisons. Low prices significantly boost purchasing power.

2. **The gap is narrowing fast** — Bulgaria gained 29 index points since 2010, second only to Romania (+33). At current pace, convergence to EU average by ~2037.

3. **Western Europe is stagnating or declining** — Sweden (-14), Italy (-12), Greece (-12), Denmark (-11) have all fallen relative to EU average since 2010.

4. **Bulgaria trails Romania by 14 points** — Romania (88) has pulled ahead, now closer to Portugal (85) than to Bulgaria (74).

5. **Monthly consumption gap to EU: €602** — This is the tangible difference in living standards, representing less healthcare, housing, food, and services consumed.

---

*Note: PPS (Purchasing Power Standard) adjusts for price differences between countries. €1 PPS buys the same basket of goods/services in any country. Bulgaria's price level is ~60% of EU average, so €1 earned in Bulgaria buys ~67% more than €1 earned in Germany.*
